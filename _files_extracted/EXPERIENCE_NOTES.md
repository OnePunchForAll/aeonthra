# EXPERIENCE_NOTES — AEONTHRA Learning Platform

## What This Product Is

AEONTHRA is a deterministic, offline-capable learning platform for college-level ethics coursework. It teaches concepts, tests understanding, tracks memory formation, guides writing, and makes a stressed student feel like they have a genuine advantage over the textbook alone.

It is not a chatbot. It is not a quiz generator. It is not gamified nonsense.

It is a learning instrument.

---

## The Intended Experience, Minute by Minute

### Arrival (0:00)
The student opens the app. The screen is dark — not dead, but alive with two slow-drifting ambient light orbs. Four large quadrant tiles fill the viewport:

- ⚡ Quick Session
- 📝 Assignment Prep
- 🧠 Deep Understanding
- 🌊 Calm Start

A frosted-glass panel floats at center with the AEONTHRA name, the course title, and a single calming sentence. When the student hovers a tile, the other three fade to 25% opacity. Their choice tells the app what kind of session this is.

The student clicks. A ripple of color radiates from their exact click point and fills the tile. The app transitions in.

**What the student feels:** "This thing already knows I'm a person, not a data point."

### First Thirty Seconds (0:00–0:30)
The home screen greets them with adaptive language based on their progress level. Not "Welcome to PHI 208" but:

- *"Let's get started. Every expert started at zero."*
- *"You're making real progress. The concepts are starting to connect."*
- *"You're in strong shape. A few more sessions and you'll be fully prepared."*

A progress ring shows overall mastery. Quick-path buttons offer immediate actions. The "Your Next Win" card shows the most urgent assignment with:
- What it's REALLY asking
- Which concepts the student already knows (✓)
- Which they still need (→ with time estimate)
- One button: "Prepare now (10 min)"

**What the student feels:** "I know exactly what to do next, and it feels achievable."

### Learning (0:30–10:00)
The student enters the Neural Forge. A large phase icon with SVG mastery ring sits at center. Six phases stretch across a connected path below:

**Orient → Apply → Recall → Prove → Reinforce → Attribute**

Each phase has its own color, icon, and purpose. The student starts with Orient — four cards of increasing depth that teach the concept before any testing. The last card reveals the common mistake and the memory hook.

In Recall, T/F questions arrive. If the student is struggling (3+ consecutive misses), the interface quietly shifts: the card glow turns calming blue, hints appear before each question, and a recovery prompt offers to review the concept. If the student is in flow (3+ streak, fast answers), the card glows brighter cyan, feedback becomes "🔥 Yes!", and explanations shorten.

The student never notices the adaptation. They just feel like the app is paying attention.

Ghosts surface: *"👻 This concept tripped you up before. Watch for: students confuse it with selfishness."* Past mistakes become fuel for reinforcement.

**What the student feels:** "I'm not just memorizing. Something is shifting inside my understanding."

### Session Complete (10:00)
The final MC question is answered. The screen expands with `completionBloom`. A floating 🔥🏆🔥 appears with "Session Complete!" in gradient text. Four stat columns show: Correct, Wrong, Streak, Mastery.

Below that, a Memory Imprint card shows the concept's core definition and memory hook, framed by the current memory stage (💎 Crystallized, 🟢 Stable, 🔵 Forming, 🟠 Fragile). This is the last thing the student sees before moving on — the essential idea, crystallized.

**What the student feels:** "I actually learned something. I can feel it sticking."

### Assignment Preparation (when needed)
The student opens an assignment. Instead of a prompt dump, they see a readiness laboratory:

- 🎯 What It's Really Asking (plain-language translation)
- 🔑 What Instructors Secretly Care About
- 🧠 Concept Readiness (memory stage icons, mastery bars, "Learn →" buttons)
- ⚡ Fastest Path to Ready (time estimate, one-click launch)
- ⚠ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- 📋 What Your Response Needs

Then the Argument Forge: Thesis → Outline → Draft. The student picks a thesis template matched to the demand type, builds a paragraph skeleton with labeled blocks, and writes with their outline visible on the left.

**What the student feels:** "I'm not staring at a blank page. I know what to say and how to structure it."

### Distinction Work (when ready)
The student enters the Gym. Six concept pairs, four exercise modes:

- **Concept Borders** — learn exactly where one idea stops and another begins
- **Corruption Cards** — spot the one-word swap that changes everything
- **Wrong Neighbors** — identify claims that sound like one concept but belong to another
- **Which Is Less Wrong?** — learn why wrong answers are tempting

**What the student feels:** "I don't just know the labels. I actually understand the differences."

### Oracle Consultation (when curious)
The student brings a question to the Tribunal. Six philosophical minds respond, each with their own color, relevance score, non-negotiable principle, and textbook source. In Challenge mode, the student submits a thesis and watches each philosopher attack it from their framework.

**What the student feels:** "I just had a conversation with six of the most important thinkers in ethics. And I understood all of them."

## Systems Running Beneath the Surface

### Flow-State Conductor
Tracks answer speed, streak, consecutive misses, accuracy. Infers the student's state (cold, warming, flow, struggling, recovering, cruising). Adapts card glow, feedback intensity, explanation density, hint visibility, and recovery prompts. Invisible to the student. They just feel understood.

### Memory Fossilization
Five stages per concept: Unseen → Fragile → Forming → Stable → Crystallized. Tracked by answer history, not just score. Ghosts of past mistakes resurface at the right moment. Memory imprint cards appear after concept completion. The app remembers how you learned, not just what you scored.

### Question Generation Engine
Deterministic generation from concept data — cross-concept comparisons, keyword claims, pitfall questions, distinction MC. Scales from 5 to 100 questions per set. No API dependency.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm before challenge.** The app orients before it tests. Every learning sequence starts with understanding, not a pop quiz.

2. **One next step.** The home screen always shows exactly one clear action. Not a dashboard of options — a single "do this now."

3. **Support before discouragement.** Recovery prompts arrive at 3 misses, not at 10. Hints appear when the student is cold. The app intervenes early.

4. **Memory over score.** A concept can be "Forming" at 40% or "Stable" at 65%. The stage matters more than the number.

5. **Borders over labels.** The Distinction Gym exists because knowing definitions is not enough. Students fail on borders — the Gym teaches borders.

6. **Writing is thinking.** The Argument Forge doesn't generate essays. It helps the student think: thesis → outline → draft.

7. **The interface is the teacher.** The glow that brightens when you're in flow. The calm blue when you're struggling. The ghost that says "this tripped you up before." The app teaches through its behavior, not just its content.

## What Makes This Product Different From Everything Else

Most learning tools are quiz machines with a progress bar. This product is a learning environment with:
- a concept engine that teaches before testing
- a flow conductor that adapts to your state
- a memory system that tracks how knowledge forms
- a distinction gym that teaches concept borders
- an argument forge that turns concepts into claims
- an oracle tribunal where philosophical minds respond to your questions
- a course atlas that shows your entire learning terrain
- an assignment lab that translates what work really demands
- a reward system that makes progress feel earned
- a welcome ritual that meets you where you are

No single feature makes it special. The integration of ALL of them — working together, referring to the same data, adapting to the same student — is what makes it feel like a place instead of a tool.
